K-Means Clustering¶

  • Plot the graph of k(no.of clusters) vs SSE
  • SSE is sum of squared distances of data points to their closest cluster center
  • Choose appropriate k where error is minimum or significantlly not reducing further
In [8]:
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans

x=data.iloc[:,[0,1,2,3]].values

# using the elbow method to find the optimal no. of clusters

ls=[]
for i in range(1,11):
    km=KMeans(n_clusters=i)
    km.fit(x)
    ls.append(km.inertia_) #sum of squared distances of samples to their closest cluster center

plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))    
plt.plot(range(1,11),ls)
plt.title("The Elbow Method")
plt.xlabel("Number of clusters")
plt.ylabel("SSE")
plt.show()

The points where the SSE reduces or the point where you can see the elbow shaped portion,those will be the ideal values of no. of clusters.So in above case 2 and 3 are the no. of clusters.Among them we choose the ideal value as 3 beacsue, after 3 there is not much of drastic change in SSE.¶
